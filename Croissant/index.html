  • Marie Antoinette did not popularize the croissant

    • Popular legend credits the French Queen and Austrian native, Marie Antoinette (1755-1793) with introducing the kipferl, and thus the croissant, to France. Homesick for the taste of her native Vienna after her marriage to King Louis XVI of France, it is said that she introduced the kipferl to Versailles when she asked the court bakers to make her the sweet pastry. However, food historians see no evidence to support this notion and agree that the baked good only became popular in France during the 19th century.
